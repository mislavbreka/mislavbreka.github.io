What is electricity?

Electricity is the movement of electrons. Electrons create charge, which we can harness to do work. All of our electronic devices operate using the same basic power source: the movement of electrons.

The three basic principles can be explained using electrons, or more specifically, the charge they create:

  • Voltage is the difference in charge between two points.
  • Current is the rate at which charge is flowing.
  • Resistance is a material's tendency to resist the flow of charge.

Ohm's law:

Ohm’s principal discovery was that the amount of electric current through a metal conductor in a circuit is directly proportional to the voltage impressed across it. Ohm expressed his discovery in the form of a simple equation, describing how voltage, current, and resistance interrelate:

VOLTAGE = CURRENT × RESISTANCE

Voltage ( E )

E = I × R

Current ( I )

I = E ÷ R

Resistance ( R )

R = E ÷ I
